# Introduction to Classical Planning & Reinforcement Learning

## 1. What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) aims to build systems that can make intelligent decisions and act effectively in an environment.

A common definition is:

> The science of making machines do things that would require intelligence if done by humans.

Modern AI focuses more on:

- Acting rationally  
- Maximizing performance  
- Making decisions under uncertainty  

---

## 2. Agents and Decision Making

An agent is an entity that:

- Receives observations from the environment  
- Takes actions to influence the environment  

A rational agent chooses actions that maximize expected performance.

---

## 3. Classical Planning

Classical planning is a model-based approach:

- The environment is known  
- Actions are deterministic  
- The goal is explicitly defined  

The objective is to find a sequence of actions that transforms the initial state into the goal state.

---

## 4. Reinforcement Learning (RL)

Reinforcement Learning is a learning-based approach:

- The model is unknown  
- The agent learns through interaction  
- The objective is to maximize cumulative reward  

Key components include:

- State  
- Action  
- Reward  
- Policy  

---

## 5. Planning vs Reinforcement Learning

| Planning | Reinforcement Learning |
|----------|----------------------|
| Model known | Model unknown |
| Uses search | Uses interaction |
| Goal-driven | Reward-driven |
| Often deterministic | Often probabilistic |

---

## 6. Markov Property and MDP

The Markov Property states:

> The future depends only on the current state, not the past.

A Markov Decision Process (MDP) includes:

- States  
- Actions  
- Transition function  
- Reward function  

---

## 7. Sequential Decision Making

In real-world problems:

- Actions have long-term consequences  
- Rewards may be delayed  
- Trade-offs between short-term and long-term exist  

---

## 8. My Thoughts

This lecture introduces the foundations of AI and highlights two main paradigms:

- Planning (model-based)  
- Reinforcement Learning (experience-based)  

Understanding the difference between them is essential for later topics such as heuristic search and MDPs.

---

## 9. Summary

- AI focuses on rational decision making  
- Planning uses known models  
- RL learns through interaction  
- MDP provides a unified framework
